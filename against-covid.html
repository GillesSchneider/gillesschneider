AI AGAINST COVID19

Screening X-ray images for COVID-19 Infections

Organized by: IEEE SIGHT MTL, VIP lab, DarwinAI.


Challenge Overview

The coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) pandemic, caused by the severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) virus, has generated an unprecedented global health crisis, with more than 2.7 million deaths worldwide. The challenge consists of designing robust machine learning algorithms to predict if the subjects of study are either COVID-19 positive or COVID-19 negative. See more here.

  • My motivations

    The SARS-CoV-2 2019 (COVID-19) current infection or past infection can be respectively detected by a viral test and an antibody test. However, artificial intelligence could become handy to help researchers diagnose COVID-10 with X-ray images, accelerating the treatment of people infected and saving lives. I am highly concerned of improving well-being and I have an in-depth knowledge of Deep Neural Network for image classification, thus I picked this challenge.

  • Dataset: COVIDx CXR-2

    The COVIDx CXR-2 dataset contains images of COVID positive and negative chest radiography images.The data distribution is given below:


    Type COVID-19 Negative COVID-19 Positive Total
    train 13793 2158 15951
    test 200 200 400

    COVIDx CXR-2 is highly imbalanced. It might be explained by the percent positive, (percentage of all coronavirus tests performed that are actually positive) that is often low. For instance, a reasonable percent positive has to be below 5% according to the World Health Organization.

  • Model

    A ResNeXt50 has been used for the classification task. We chose this network because it is a robust and highly modularized network architecture.

  • Training

    We created a custom balanced train set from the original train set of COVIDx CXR-2 by including all the COVID-19 Positive CXRs (2158) and randomly picking the same number of COVID-19 Negative CXRs (2158). We used the original test set as the validation set.


    Type COVID-19 Negative COVID-19 Positive Image Size Total

    test 200 200 240x240 400

  • Results

    The score of the challenge to the final test results is based on the weighted sum of the Sensitivity, Positive Predictive Value (PPV), and the Runtime of the model. To be more specific, the final score is as follows:

    Score=6SP+5SN+3Pp+2PN-wt


    Where SP and Pp refer to the Sensitivity and PPV of the COVID-19 positive class. PNand SN refers to the Sensitivity and PPV of the COVID-19 negative class. t is the runtime of the algorithm and w is a non-negative weight. The score can be subject to change before the opening of the competition.
